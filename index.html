Mutual Aid

Who We Are

The purpose of this spreadsheet is to make mutual aid in our community easier so that funds get to those that need it. This is made primarily with Cornell students in mind, but we welcome members of the larger Ithaca community to participate and use this form as you need. See the contact section and reach out to Cornell Abolitionist Revolutionary Society (CARS) if you have questions.

This tool was heavily inspired by Students Organizing Mutual Aid and Defense's (SOMAD) reparations spreadsheet. If y'all are reading this thank you! You can check them out here

Need Funds?

Fill out the form below!

How does the form work? We do our best to anonymize the responses (Jane Doe will appear as JaDo) and give you the option to put down a friends cash link if you're not comfortable putting your own. Your name and cash information will appear on the next sheet over.

The form is not an application, anyone who signs up will be put on our spreadsheet. However, BIPOC, low-income, and trans and GNC students will be placed higher on the sheet for priority. If you do not belong to any of those groups, we ask that you not misuse the system and only sign up if you truly are in need.

Redistribute Your Money

Take a Look at the Sheet Below

Have access to wealth or some extra money in your account? Take a look at the next sheet (take a look at the bottom tabs) and you'll see a list of cashlinks of community members in need of some funds. Take into consideration that the order is not necessarily chronological but as explained above.

According to Resource Generation, people in the top 10% should start the journey of redistributing stolen wealth by redistributing 10% of their wealth monthly, with the goal of redistributing all or almost all inherited wealth over time.
